IMCD Phosphoproteome with Acute Lithium Treatment

This phosphoproteome database is based on protein mass spectrometry data from the NHLBI Epithelial Systems Biology Laboratory (ESBL). All data are from inner medullary collecting duct (IMCD) isolated from rat kidneys 9 hours after an oral administration of LiCl or NaCl. Reference: Trepiccione F, Pisitkun T, Hoffert JD, Poulsen SB, Capasso G, Nielsen S, Knepper MA, Fenton RA, Christensen BM. Early targets of lithium in rat kidney inner medullary collecting duct include p38 and ERK1/2. Kidney Int. 2014 Oct;86(4):757-67. PMID: 24786704.

Brief methods: IMCD was isolated from rat kidneys 9 hours after a gavage of LiCl or NaCl. Samples were reduced, alkylated, and digested by trypsin. Phosphopeptides were enriched using Ga3+-IMAC columns, and dried samples were resuspended in 0.1% formic acid and desalted before analysis by LC-MS/MS. Tryptic peptides were analyzed on an LTQ Orbitrap mass spectrometer equipped with a nano-electrospray ion source. The resulting MS spectra were searched using three search algorithms: SEQUEST, InsPecT, and OMSSA. Label-free quantification of MS1 peaks was carried out by QUOIL software. Relative abundance of LiCl versus NaCl treated groups was expressed as log2 ratio. Phosphorylation site certainty was established as Phosphate Localization Score (PLS) = 7 or Ascore = 19 or by the simultaneous identification of the same phosphorylation sites from the three search algorithms.
