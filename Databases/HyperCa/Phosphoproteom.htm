IMCD Phosphoproteomics Data on Hypercalcemia Induced Nephrogenic Diabetes Insipidus

This database represents renal inner medullary collecting duct (IMCD) proteins and phosphoproteins from PTH treated rats and controls at the early stage of hypercalcemia-induced NDI. Male Sprague-Dawley rats were treated with PTH (15 μg/kg/day) or vehicle via osmotic minipump for 1 day. All data are from IMCD cells freshly isolated from rat renal inner medullas.

Brief methods: Samples were analyzed using LTQ Orbitrap XL mass spectrometer equipped with nanoflow ultra-high pressure liquid chromatography system. The mass spectra were analyzed using two search algorithms, SEQUEST (Bioworks version 3.31 SP1) and InsPecT (version 20070905).

The work was performed at the Faculty of Medicine, Thammasat University, and the Faculty of Medicine, Chulalongkorn University, Thailand. This database was created by Sookkasem Khositseth and Trairak Pisitkun. Contact us for any questions: pisitkut@nhlbi.nih.gov Database is hosted by the Epithelial Systems Biology Laboratory (Mark Knepper, Chief) at the National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute as part of its Kidney Systems Biology Project.

Reference: Khositseth S, Charngkaew K, Boonkrai C, Somparn P, Uawithya P, Chomanee N, Payne DM, Fenton RA, Pisitkun T. Hypercalcemia induces targeted autophagic degradation of aquaporin-2 at the onset of nephrogenic diabetes insipidus. Kidney Int. 2017 May;91(5):1070-1087. PMID:28139295
