Comprehensive Proteomic Analysis of Rat Renal Inner Medullary Collecting Duct Nuclei: Transcription Factors

This database reports all transcription factors identified by mass spectrometry (LC-MS/MS) in the nuclear extract and/or nuclear pellet of inner medullary collecting duct (IMCD) cells freshly isolated from rat kidneys (NE-PER kit, Thermo). Two samples were analyzed for each fraction, one from IMCD cells treated for 30 minutes with a vasopressin analog (dDAVP, 1 nM) and one from IMCD cells treated with the vehicle (control, CT). This database was created by Christina Pickering, Cameron Grady, Chung-Lin Chou, Pablo Sandoval, and Mark Knepper of the Systems Biology Center, Division of Intramural Research, NHLBI, NIH, Bethesda, Maryland.

Total number of transcription factors identified = 369. Transcription factors identified in Extract and Pellet = 159; transcription factors in Extract only = 122; proteins in Pellet only = 88.

Click on the Gene Symbol to reveal Virtual Western Blots for each protein in control samples, mapping MS data to SDS-PAGE gel slices from which they were identified.

The database of all proteins identified in the Extract and Pellet is available here. The list of all proteins identified on the basis of at least two distinct peptides is available here.

Difference in spectral counts (SC) was tested using the Fisher Exact Test relative to the total spectral counts for all proteins (Extract: CT, 110787; dDAVP, 108171; Pellet: CT, 138631; dDAVP, 142352). For the nuclear extract and nuclear pellet fractions, the p-values represent the probability that a difference in spectral counts between control and dDAVP samples could have been obtained by random selection from all spectra found in the respective fraction.
