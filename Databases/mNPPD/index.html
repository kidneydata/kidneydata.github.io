mpkCCD Nuclear Phosphoproteome - Response to Vasopressin

This database of mpkCCD nuclear proteins is based on protein mass spectrometry data from the NHLBI Epithelial Systems Biology Laboratory (ESBL). All data are from cultured mpkCCD clone 11 cells. This clonal line was developed to maximize AQP2 response to the vasopressin analog 1-desamino-8-D-arginine vasopressin (dDAVP) both in terms of membrane trafficking and transcription. Please cite: Bolger SJ et al. Quantitative phosphoproteomics in nuclei of vasopressin-sensitive renal collecting duct cells. Am J Physiol Cell Physiol. 2012; 303: C1006-20. PMID: 22992673.

Brief methods: mpkCCD cells (clone 11) were fractionated using the commercially available NE-PER kit (Thermo Scientific) yielding the nuclear extract (NE) and nuclear pellet (NP). Protein samples were reduced, alkylated, and digested with trypsin, and the resulting peptides were separated into 24 fractions using strong cation exchange chromatography. The fractionated samples were dried in vacuo and resuspended in 0.1 % formic acid. Phosphopeptides were enriched using Fe-NTA phosphopeptide IMAC columns, and dried samples were resuspended in 0.1% formic acid and desalted using Graphite Spin columns before analysis by LC-MS/MS. Tryptic peptides were analyzed on an LTQ Orbitrap Velos mass spectrometer equipped with a nano-electrospray ion source. The peptides were fractionated with a reversed-phase PicoFrit column using a linear gradient of 5%–35% ACN in 0.1% FA in 45 min at a flow rate of 0.25 μl/min. Precursor mass spectra (MS1) were acquired in the orbitrap at 60,000 resolution and product mass spectra (MS2) were acquired with the ion trap.
