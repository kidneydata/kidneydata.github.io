Rat Inner Medullary Collecting Duct Phosphotyrosine Database

This database of renal inner medullary collecting duct phosphotyrosine is based on protein mass spectrometry data from the NHLBI Epithelial Systems Biology Laboratory (ESBL). All data are from IMCD cells freshly purified from rat inner medullas.

Brief methods: IMCD suspension was treated with 100 μM pervanadate, 1 mM vanadate, 180 μM H2O2, or 100 μM pervanadate with 100 μg/ml catalase for 10 min. Each sample was digested with trypsin followed by anti-phosphotyrosine peptide immunoprecipitation using PY100 and PY66 antibodies. Phosphopeptide enrichment using immobilized metal affinity chromatography (IMAC) with Ga3+ matrix was performed either before or after the immunoprecipitation procedure. Mass spectrometry was performed using an LTQ Orbitrap XL.

Reference: Zhao et al., Large-scale phosphotyrosine proteomic profiling of rat renal collecting duct epithelium reveals predominance of proteins involved in cell polarity determination. Am J Physiol Cell Physiol. 2012 Jan;302(1):C27-45.

This website was created by Boyang Zhao, Trairak Pisitkun, and Mark Knepper. Contact us with questions or comments: knep@helix.nih.gov

Database updated: August 4, 2011 (download data)

Notations: * = Phosphorylated amino acid. @ = Methionine oxidation. † = Novel phosphorylation site. Phosphosite Assignment Certainty was determined based on the PhosphoScore for SEQUEST search algorithm and/or the Phosphate Localization Score (PLS) for the Inspect search algorithm. Since there is no scoring algorithm for the OMSSA search algorithm, phosphopeptides identified only by OMSSA were labeled "ambiguous" unless there is only one choice for the phosphorylation site.
